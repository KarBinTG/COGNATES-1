What Is Extended Reality?

Extended Reality (XR)

An umbrella term covering ALL immersive technologies that merge the physical and digital worlds, including Virtual Reality (VR), Augmented Reality (AR), and Mixed Reality (MR). XR extends human experience by altering our perception of the environment.

17

Mixed Reality (MR)

Digital objects that are AWARE of and INTERACT with the real world. Not just overlaid on top, but anchored to physical surfaces, responding to real objects.

AR: Pokemon appears floating in your room (doesn't know about your table).
MR: A virtual ball bounces OFF your real table and rolls under your real chair. The digital understands the physical.

Degrees of Freedom (DoF)

3DoF (Rotation Only)

  • Look up/down, left/right, tilt
  • Can't move position in space
  • Like sitting in a chair and looking around
  • Example: Google Cardboard, 360° videos

6DoF (Rotation + Position)

  • Look AND move in any direction
  • Walk, crouch, lean, reach
  • Like moving freely in a room
  • Example: Quest 3, HoloLens, Vision Pro

Why XR Improves Learning

  • Experiential: learn by DOING, not just reading (retention +75%)
  • Safe failure: make mistakes without real-world consequences
  • Engagement: immersion increases attention and motivation
  • Accessibility: "visit" places you can't physically reach
  • Visualization: see abstract concepts as 3D objects

32

Ethical Concerns

  • Privacy: headsets have cameras, eye tracking, biometric data
  • Addiction: immersive worlds may encourage escapism
  • Harassment: virtual spaces enable new forms of abuse
  • Reality confusion: blurring lines between real and virtual (children)
  • Data collection: eye tracking reveals interests, emotions, health
  • Digital divide: expensive tech widens inequality

Key Takeaways

  • XR = umbrella term (VR + AR + MR)
  • VR = total immersion, AR = overlay on real, MR = digital interacts with real
  • Enabled by: computer vision, tracking, 3D rendering, AI
  • Applications: education, healthcare, training, retail, entertainment
  • Benefits are real but so are cost, health, and ethical concerns
