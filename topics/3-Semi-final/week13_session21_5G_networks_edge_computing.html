What Makes 5G Different?

Three Pillars of 5G

1. Enhanced Mobile Broadband (eMBB): Blazing fast speeds (up to 20 Gbps). Download a full movie in 3 seconds.

2. Ultra-Reliable Low Latency (URLLC): Near-instant response (1ms). Critical for self-driving cars, remote surgery.

3. Massive Machine-Type Comm (mMTC): Connect 1 million devices per km². Enable massive IoT deployments.

What Is Edge Computing?

Process data NEAR the source, not in a distant cloud

Instead of sending all data to a cloud server far away (adding latency), edge computing processes data at or near the device that generates it. Combines the power of cloud with the speed of local processing.

14

Cloud vs Edge

Cloud Computing

  • Data sent to distant data center
  • High latency (50-200ms round trip)
  • Unlimited processing power
  • Requires strong internet connection
  • Best for: big analytics, storage, non-urgent

Edge Computing

  • Data processed locally or nearby
  • Ultra-low latency (1-10ms)
  • Limited but sufficient processing
  • Works with intermittent connectivity
  • Best for: real-time decisions, IoT, autonomous
15

Why 5G + Edge Together?

5G provides the PIPE. Edge provides the PROCESSING. Together: real-time intelligence everywhere.

  • 5G connects massive devices to the network (fast, low-latency)
  • Edge processes data nearby (no waiting for distant cloud)
  • Result: instant decisions at the point of action
  • Example: traffic camera detects accident → edge processes → signals change in 1ms

Separating facts from fear

26

5G Myths vs Facts

MythFact
"5G causes COVID-19"Completely false. Radio waves don't spread viruses. Debunked by WHO, CDC, and all scientists.
"5G is dangerous radiation"5G uses non-ionizing radiation (like FM radio). Decades of research: no health harm at regulated levels.
"5G replaces WiFi"No. They complement each other. WiFi for indoor, 5G for outdoor/mobile.
"5G is available everywhere"Coverage is limited to cities. Rural areas may wait years.
27

Legitimate 5G Concerns

  • Digital divide: 5G in cities only widens urban-rural gap
  • Surveillance: more connectivity = more data collection potential
  • Cybersecurity: more connected devices = larger attack surface
  • E-waste: billions of 4G devices becoming obsolete
  • Energy consumption: more towers = more electricity needed
  • Cost: 5G plans more expensive for consumers
