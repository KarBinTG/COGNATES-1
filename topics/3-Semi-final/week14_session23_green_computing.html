What Is Green Computing?

Green Computing (Green IT)

The practice of designing, manufacturing, using, and disposing of computing resources in ways that minimize environmental impact. Covers the ENTIRE lifecycle: from raw material extraction to end-of-life recycling. Goals: reduce energy consumption, minimize e-waste, lower carbon emissions.

E-Waste Crisis

  • 62 million TONS of e-waste generated annually (2024)
  • Only 22% properly recycled; rest goes to landfills
  • Contains toxic materials: lead, mercury, cadmium, flame retardants
  • Average smartphone lifespan: 2.5 years (replaced, not broken)
  • Philippines: 3rd largest e-waste generator in Southeast Asia
  • Most PH e-waste ends up in informal recycling (health hazards)

15

PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness)

PUE = Total Facility Energy / IT Equipment Energy

PUE of 1.0 = perfect (all energy goes to computing). PUE of 2.0 = half the energy wasted on cooling/lighting. Industry average: 1.58. Google/Meta: 1.1. Ideal target: below 1.3.

Every 0.1 reduction in PUE saves millions of dollars and tons of CO2 annually for large data centers.

16

Personal Green Computing

  • Extend device lifespan (don't upgrade every year if unnecessary)
  • Enable power management (sleep mode, auto-brightness)
  • Reduce streaming quality when not needed (720p vs 4K)
  • Delete unused apps, files, emails (less cloud storage = less energy)
  • Recycle old devices properly (not in regular trash)
  • Use WiFi over mobile data (WiFi is more energy-efficient)

E-Waste Solutions

Reduce & Reuse

  • Extend device life (repair, not replace)
  • Buy refurbished devices
  • Donate working old devices
  • Right-to-repair legislation
  • Modular design (replace parts, not whole device)

Recycle Properly

  • Take-back programs (Samsung, Apple)
  • Certified e-waste recyclers
  • Urban mining (extract valuable metals)
  • Proper disposal of batteries
  • Never burn or throw in regular trash

The Carbon Footprint of the Web

  • Average webpage: 2MB size, ~0.5g CO2 per visit
  • Heavy webpage: 5MB+, 1.5g CO2 per visit
  • 1 million visits to a heavy site: 1.5 TONS of CO2
  • Streaming 1 hour of Netflix: ~36g CO2
  • Sending 1 email with attachment: ~50g CO2
  • Green web design: lighter pages = less energy = less CO2

The Paradox

Technology is BOTH the problem AND part of the solution.

Data centers consume massive energy, BUT cloud computing is 3x more efficient than on-premises. AI training emits tons of CO2, BUT AI optimizes energy grids saving even more. The key: use technology WISELY and EFFICIENTLY.
