What Is a Robot?

Robot

A programmable machine capable of carrying out a series of actions automatically. Robots can sense their environment, process information, and take physical actions. They range from simple (Roomba vacuum) to complex (surgical robots, humanoids).

Cobots (Collaborative Robots)

Human + Robot = Cobot

Designed to work ALONGSIDE humans safely, not in cages behind fences. They sense human presence, slow down or stop if contacted. Combine robot precision with human judgment and flexibility.

Example: A cobot holds a heavy car door perfectly still while a human worker attaches bolts. Neither could do it as well alone.

12

Traditional vs Cobot

Traditional Industrial Robot

  • Works in caged-off area
  • Dangerous if human enters zone
  • Fixed programming
  • Expensive ($100K-500K)
  • Needs specialized programmers
  • Best for: high-volume, same task

Collaborative Robot (Cobot)

  • Works next to humans safely
  • Stops on contact (force sensors)
  • Easy to reprogram (drag-and-teach)
  • Affordable ($25K-50K)
  • Workers can teach it new tasks
  • Best for: flexible, mixed tasks

RPA (Software Robots)

Software "bots" that mimic human actions on computers: clicking, typing, copying data. No physical robot involved, just automated workflows.

Amazon: 750,000+ Robots

  • Kiva robots: carry shelves TO workers (not workers TO shelves)
  • Sparrow: AI-powered robot picks individual items from bins
  • Robin: sorts packages by destination
  • Result: orders packed in minutes, not hours
  • But: Amazon ALSO hired 1.5M humans alongside robots
  • Lesson: robots handle repetitive; humans handle exceptions

Key Takeaways

  • Robots: industrial, service, medical, cobots, autonomous vehicles, humanoid
  • Automation spectrum: physical robots → RPA → intelligent → hyperautomation
  • Cobots: safe human-robot collaboration (the future of work)
  • AI enables: vision, learning, navigation, prediction in robots
  • WEF: 85M jobs displaced BUT 97M new ones created
  • PH opportunity: RPA in BPO, gradual manufacturing automation
