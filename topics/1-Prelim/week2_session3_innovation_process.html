04Overview

The 8 Stages

1. Problem→2. Research→3. Ideate→4. Prototype→5. Test→6. Iterate→7. Implement→8. Evaluate
05Stage 1

Problem Identification

01
Discover a real pain point
"What problem are we solving?" Must be real, significant, experienced by actual people.

Rule: Can't state it in one sentence? Not ready to build.

06Stage 2

Research & Exploration

02
Study context, users, existing solutions
"Who is affected? What exists already?"
07Stage 3

Ideation

03
Generate multiple possible solutions
Quantity before quality. Wild ideas welcome.
08Stage 4

Prototyping

04
Build a simple, testable version
NOT finished product. Rough, fast, cheap.
TypeToolTime
Paper sketchPen + paperMinutes
Digital mockupFigma, CanvaHours
MVPCode/no-codeDays-Weeks
09Stage 5

Testing & Feedback

05
Let real users try it
"Does this actually work for them?"

Golden Rule: You are NOT your user. What makes sense to you may confuse others.

10Stage 6

Iteration

06
Improve based on feedback
Cycle stages 4-5-6 multiple times. That's normal.

Innovation is NOT linear. Expect loops.

11Stage 7-8

Implement & Evaluate

07: Implement

Launch refined solution. Train, migrate, support.

08: Evaluate

Measure impact. Did it create intended value?

13Critical

Problem-First
vs.
Solution-First

14Two Paths

Which Do You Take?

✓ Problem-First

  1. Discover real pain
  2. Research deeply
  3. Find tech that fits
  4. Build and test

→ High adoption

❌ Solution-First

  1. See cool tech
  2. Get excited
  3. Hunt for problems
  4. Build without validation

→ Nobody needs it

16Bad Example

Solution-First Failure

19Deep Dive

Why
Innovations Fail

20Top 5

Failure Reasons

  1. Wrong problem – nobody asked for it
  2. No research – assumed, not asked
  3. Over-engineering – too complex
  4. Wrong context – ignored infra/culture
  5. Scaled too fast – grew before validating
21Case

Google Glass (2013)

Happened

  • $1,500 smart glasses
  • No clear everyday problem
  • Made others uncomfortable

Why Failed

  • Solution-first thinking
  • Ignored social context
  • No daily-life research

25Deep Dive

Prototyping
& MVP

26Why

Benefits of Prototyping

  • Fail fast, fail cheap
  • Make ideas tangible
  • Get honest feedback
  • Align the team
  • Reduce risk
27Fidelity

Low to High

LevelTypeBest For
LowPaper sketchEarly concepts
MediumWireframe (Figma)Layout testing
HighClickable mockupUX testing
FunctionalMVP (code)Market validation
28MVP

Minimum Viable Product

MVP = simplest version that tests core idea with real users. ONE feature solving the main problem.

MVP ✓

Grab v1: book, see driver, pay.

NOT MVP ✗

Rides+food+wallet+rewards before launch.

29Loop

Build-Measure-Learn

BUILD→MEASURE→LEARN→REPEAT

Faster cycles = faster improvement.
