Algorithmic Bias

Definition: AI systems systematically produce unfair outcomes that discriminate against certain groups, usually because of biased training data.

Why It Happens

  • Training data reflects historical discrimination
  • Developers don't test across diverse groups
  • Feedback loops amplify initial bias
  • Proxy variables correlate with protected attributes

Why It's Dangerous

  • AI SCALES bias to millions instantly
  • Decisions appear "objective"
  • Difficult to detect without auditing
  • Affects hiring, lending, policing, healthcare

Real Cases of AI Bias

SystemBias FoundImpact
Amazon Hiring AIPenalized female-associated keywordsWomen systematically rejected
Facial Recognition34% error dark-skinned women vs 0.8% light-skinned menWrongful arrests
COMPAS (Courts)Higher risk scores for Black defendantsLonger sentences

Deepfakes 🎭

Deepfakes: AI-generated realistic fake videos, audio, or images of anyone, increasingly indistinguishable from real content.

Dangerous Uses

  • CEO voice clone ($243K stolen 2019)
  • Political manipulation during elections
  • Non-consensual content (96% of deepfakes)
  • Scam calls impersonating family
  • Fake celebrity endorsements

Core Danger

Erodes trust in ALL media.

"Liar's dividend": guilty claim real evidence is fake.

500%

increase 2023-2024

The Privacy Paradox

People SAY privacy matters but ACT as if it doesn't.

What We Say

  • "Privacy is important to me"
  • "Companies shouldn't collect my data"
  • "I'm concerned about surveillance"

What We Do

  • Accept terms without reading (150+/year)
  • Share freely on social media
  • Use "free" services (you ARE the product)
  • Install apps with excessive permissions
