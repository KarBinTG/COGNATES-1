What Is Design Thinking?

Design Thinking: A human-centered, iterative problem-solving approach that focuses on understanding user needs, challenging assumptions, redefining problems, and creating innovative solutions through prototyping and testing.

Origin

  • Stanford d.school (Hasso Plattner Institute)
  • IDEO consulting firm (Tim Brown)
  • Formalized in 1990s-2000s

Who Uses It

  • Apple, Google, Airbnb
  • IBM, Samsung, Netflix
  • GCash, Grab Philippines

Three Key Characteristics

👤

Human-Centered

Start with people and their real needs, not technology

🔄

Iterative

Go forward, go back, try, fail, learn, repeat. Non-linear.

💡

Creative Confidence

Structure for creativity. Everyone can innovate with the right process.

Stage 1: Empathize 👤

You cannot solve a problem you don't understand. And you cannot understand from your desk.

Methods

  • Interviews: Open questions, deep listening
  • Observation: Watch users in natural environment
  • Immersion: Experience the problem yourself
  • Empathy Maps: Document say/think/do/feel

Interview Tips

  • Ask "WHY?" five times to dig deeper
  • Never lead with your solution
  • Listen 80%, talk 20%
  • Look for emotions, not just facts
  • Ask about SPECIFIC past experiences

The Empathy Map

💬 SAYS

Direct quotes and statements

"Grabe ka dugay mag-enroll! 3 hours ko nag-line!"

🧠 THINKS

Beliefs, concerns (may not say aloud)

"Basin di ko makuha ang gusto nako nga schedule..."

👀 DOES

Observable actions, behaviors

Arrives 5am. Brings food. Checks phone repeatedly.

❤️ FEELS

Emotions, frustrations, desires

Frustrated. Anxious. Helpless. Exhausted.

💡 Insight lives in GAPS: when SAYS doesn't match DOES, that's where innovation hides.

Stage 2: Define 🎯

Problem Statement Format

[User] needs [need] because [insight].

✅ Good Problem Statement

"A CFCI student needs a faster enrollment process because the current system wastes 3 hours in queues each semester, causing stress and missed opportunities."

❌ Bad Problem Statement

"We need to build an app."

This is a SOLUTION, not a problem. No user, no need, no insight.

💡 The quality of your solution depends on the quality of your problem definition.

⏸️ Write a Problem Statement

Pick a REAL campus problem. Use the format:

[User] needs [need] because [insight].

Stage 3: Ideate 💡

Goal: Generate MANY solutions. Quantity over quality. Best ideas come after idea #20+.

6 Rules of Brainstorming

1.

Defer judgment – No "that won't work" during ideation

2.

Quantity over quality – Aim for 50+ ideas

3.

Build on others' – "Yes, and..." not "No, but..."

4.

Encourage wild ideas – Crazy is welcome

5.

One conversation – Don't talk over each other

6.

Be visual – Sketch, diagram, draw

"How Might We" Questions

Transform problems into opportunity questions that open creative space.

Problem:

"Students wait 3 hours for enrollment."

🎯

HMW reduce enrollment to under 30 minutes?

→ Speed focus
✨

HMW make waiting time productive?

→ Experience focus
🚀

HMW eliminate the need to be physically present?

→ Redesign focus

Each HMW opens DIFFERENT solution spaces → different creative directions

Case Study: Airbnb

The Problem (2009)

Revenue stuck at $200/week. Almost bankrupt. Listings weren't getting booked.

Empathize

Founders flew to NYC, stayed with hosts. Discovered: listing photos were TERRIBLE. Phone cameras, bad lighting.

Define

"Hosts need attractive listings because bad photos prevent bookings."

Prototype

Rented a camera. Took professional photos for 20 listings in NYC.

Test & Result

Those 20 listings: bookings DOUBLED.

$200/week → $400/week → eventually $100 BILLION company

All from understanding the REAL problem through empathy.

Design Thinking → Your Final Project

1
Empathize: Research a REAL community/business problem. Interview actual people.
2
Define: Clear problem statement based on research, not assumptions.
3
Ideate: Consider MULTIPLE solutions before choosing. Not just first idea.
4
Prototype: Visual mockup or working demo.
5
Test Plan: How would you validate with real users?

⚠️ Panels will ask: "Who did you talk to? What did you discover?" Do the empathy work.

Design Thinking = human-centered + iterative + structured

2.

5 stages: Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test

3.

Non-linear! Go back to any stage when needed.

4.

Starts with EMPATHY, not solutions.

5.

Problem statement: [User] needs [need] because [insight]

6.

Ideation: defer judgment, quantity > quality, "yes and..."

7.

SCAMPER + HMW = ideation power tools

Airbnb: empathy → $100B company
