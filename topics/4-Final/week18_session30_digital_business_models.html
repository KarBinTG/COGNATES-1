Network Effects 🔄

More users join → Platform MORE valuable → More users join

Virtuous cycle that creates winner-take-all dynamics

How It Works

  • Grab: more drivers → shorter wait → more riders → more drivers
  • Shopee: more sellers → more products → more buyers → more sellers
  • Facebook: more friends there → more reason to stay → more friends join

Winner-Take-All

  • Once one platform dominates, hard to compete
  • Users won't switch (their network is there)
  • Creates natural monopolies
  • That's why #2 often dies (Friendster → Facebook)

The Chicken & Egg Problem 🐔🥚

Every platform faces this: need users to attract users. How did they solve it?

PlatformCold-Start Strategy
GrabSubsidized drivers (guaranteed minimum earnings without riders)
ShopeeZero commission for sellers + free shipping for buyers (first year)
FacebookHarvard ONLY first (intense local density before expanding)
GCashPartnered with Globe (existing millions of telecom users)
AirbnbCross-posted to Craigslist (poached existing supply)
UberFree rides for riders + signing bonuses for drivers

Lesson: subsidize ONE side heavily at start to bootstrap the network.

When Network Effects Aren't Absolute

Weakening Factors

  • Multi-homing: users on BOTH platforms (Grab + Angkas)
  • Local density: only need coverage in YOUR area
  • Differentiation: serve different segments (cars vs motorcycles)
  • Regulation: government forces competition (DITO vs Globe/Smart)

Opportunity for You

  • LOCAL platforms can beat global ones in specific markets
  • GenSan-specific app > national app for local problems
  • Deep local knowledge is your unfair advantage
  • Start narrow (one city), expand later

When to Monetize

❌ Too Early

Charging before critical mass. Kills growth.

10 users won't pay for an empty platform

✅ Sweet Spot

Reach critical mass FIRST, then introduce revenue.

Users see value → willing to pay

❌ Too Late

Burn cash, die before finding revenue model.

"We'll figure it out" → bankruptcy

For final project: know your revenue model even if you wouldn't charge day one.

Unit Economics: CAC & LTV

CAC

Customer Acquisition Cost

How much to GET one customer?

Example: ₱50 in ads → 1 customer = ₱50 CAC

LTV

Lifetime Value

How much does one customer SPEND total?

Example: 3 orders/mo × ₱200 × 12 mo × 20% = ₱1,440

Golden Rule: LTV ≥ 3× CAC

If LTV < CAC → you lose money on every customer. More growth = faster death.

⏸️ Unit Economics Check

For YOUR BMC idea, estimate:

CAC: How would you acquire customers? How much per customer?

LTV: How much would one customer pay over their lifetime?

Does LTV ≥ 3× CAC?
