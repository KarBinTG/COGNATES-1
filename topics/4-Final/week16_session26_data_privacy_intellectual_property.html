RA 10173: Data Privacy Act of 2012

Purpose: Protects individual personal information in government and private sector information and communications systems in the Philippines.

Key Provisions

  • Personal Information Controller (PIC) must protect data
  • Data subjects have enforceable rights
  • Mandatory Data Protection Officer (DPO)
  • National Privacy Commission (NPC) enforces
  • Breach notification within 72 hours

Penalties

₱5M

Maximum fine

1-6 yrs

Imprisonment

Types of Personal Information

📋 Regular Personal Info

  • Name, address
  • Phone, email
  • Birthday, age
  • Any identifier

Standard protection

🔒 Sensitive Personal Info

  • Race, ethnicity
  • Health records
  • Gov't IDs (SSS, PhilHealth)
  • Education records
  • Court records

Higher protection + heavier penalties

⚖️ Privileged Info

  • Attorney-client
  • Doctor-patient
  • Other privileged communication

Highest protection

National Privacy Commission (NPC)

What NPC Does

  • Investigates complaints from citizens
  • Issues compliance orders to violators
  • Imposes fines and penalties
  • Conducts organizational audits
  • Issues advisories and guidelines
  • Registers Data Protection Officers

How to File Complaint

  • Online via NPC website
  • Written complaint with evidence
  • NPC investigates within 10 days
  • Mediation or formal hearing
  • Can result in fines + imprisonment

NPC Hotline: (02) 8234-2228

complaints@privacy.gov.ph

In PH: Copyright is AUTOMATIC upon creation. No registration needed.

Software & Copyright

Key Fact

Software is protected as a literary work under Philippine copyright law (RA 8293). Protection is automatic upon creation.

Philippine IP Framework

  • RA 8293: IP Code of PH
  • IPOPHL: enforcement agency
  • RA 10175: computer-related IP offenses
  • No registration needed for copyright

Digital Age IP Issues

  • Software piracy (cracked software)
  • AI-generated content ownership (?)
  • Open source vs proprietary licensing
  • Data ownership on platforms
  • Patent trolls
  • Fair use vs infringement

AI & Copyright Controversy 🤖

The Problem

  • AI trained on billions of copyrighted works without permission
  • Generates content "inspired by" artists' styles
  • Multiple lawsuits ongoing globally
  • NYT vs OpenAI, artists vs Stability AI

PH Status

No specific AI copyright law yet. Will be major legal issue in your career.

Unresolved Questions

  • Is AI training "fair use"?
  • Who owns AI-generated output?
  • Can AI be an "author"? (Courts say NO)
  • Does the prompter own the result?
  • What about AI mimicking specific artists?

Privacy by Design (7 Principles)

1
Proactive not reactive – Prevent, don't just remedy
2
Privacy as default – Protected without user action
3
Embedded in design – Built-in, not bolted-on
4
Full functionality – Privacy AND features, not either/or
5
End-to-end security – Protect entire data lifecycle
6
Visibility & transparency – Users can verify
7
Respect for user privacy – User-centric always

Applying Privacy by Design

Scenario: Building a Student Information System for CFCI

✅ Good Design

  • Collect ONLY necessary data
  • Sharing OFF by default (opt-in)
  • Encrypt database from day one
  • Auto-delete after retention period
  • Students can view/export their data
  • Role-based access (not everyone sees all)

❌ Bad Design

  • Collect everything "just in case"
  • Share with partners by default
  • Store passwords in plain text
  • Keep data forever
  • No way for students to see their data
  • Everyone has admin access
