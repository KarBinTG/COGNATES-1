TypeQuestionComplexityValue

06

Descriptive: What Happened?

Summarize past data. Reports, dashboards, basic statistics. Most organizations are HERE.

ExampleOutput
Monthly sales report"Revenue was ₱2.3M in June"
Website traffic dashboard"1,200 visitors yesterday"
Student grade summary"Average quiz score: 78%"
Social media metrics"Post reached 5,000 people"
07

Diagnostic: Why Did It Happen?

Dig deeper. Find root causes. Drill-down analysis, correlation discovery.

QuestionAnalysisFinding
Why did sales drop in June?Compare with promotions calendar"No promo in June; competitor had a sale"
Why low quiz scores?Topic breakdown analysis"IoT questions had 40% fail rate"
Why high cart abandonment?Funnel analysis"Users drop at payment step (too complex)"
08

Predictive: What Will Happen?

Use historical patterns to forecast the future. Machine learning, statistical models.

ScenarioPredictionHow
Customer churn"This user will cancel next month"Behavior patterns similar to past churners
Demand forecasting"Need 30% more stock in December"Historical seasonal trends
Fraud detection"This transaction is 92% likely fraud"Anomaly vs normal patterns
Grade prediction"Student at risk of failing"Attendance + quiz trends
09

Prescriptive: What Should We Do?

Recommend optimal actions. The highest level: AI tells you the BEST decision to make.

ScenarioPrescription
Customer about to churn"Offer 20% discount NOW. 73% retention rate expected."
Inventory optimization"Order 500 units of Product X by Thursday to avoid stockout."
Route optimization (Grab)"Send Driver B this route. Saves 8 minutes vs alternative."
At-risk student"Schedule tutoring session this week. Similar interventions improved grades by 15%."

13

The Dirty Secret

80% of a data analyst's time is spent on Phase 3: Data Preparation (cleaning).

Real data is MESSY. Missing values, duplicates, wrong formats, typos, inconsistencies. You fix all of that BEFORE you can analyze anything.

14

Data Cleaning Examples

ProblemExampleFix
Missing valuesPhone field empty for 200 recordsRemove or estimate
Inconsistent format"GenSan" vs "General Santos" vs "GS"Standardize to one format
DuplicatesSame student registered twiceMerge or remove
OutliersAge = 999Investigate and correct
Wrong typeDate stored as text "July 10"Convert to date format

Why Visualize?

Humans process images 60,000x faster than text.

A table of 10,000 numbers? Nobody reads that. A chart showing the trend? Understood in 2 seconds. Visualization makes data ACTIONABLE for decision-makers who don't have time to read reports.

17

22

Algorithmic Bias

AI learns from historical data. If history is biased, the AI becomes biased.

CaseBiasCause
Amazon hiring AIRejected female applicantsTrained on 10 years of male-dominated hires
Facial recognitionHigher error for dark skinTraining data mostly light-skinned faces
Loan algorithmsDeny minorities more oftenZIP code correlated with race in training data
Healthcare AIUnder-prioritized Black patientsUsed cost (not illness) as health proxy
23

Cambridge Analytica

  • Harvested data from 87 million Facebook users (without consent)
  • Built psychological profiles of every voter
  • Targeted political ads to manipulate opinions
  • Influenced 2016 US election and Brexit referendum
  • Result: Facebook fined $5 billion, company dissolved
24

Data Ethics Framework

  • Consent: Did people agree to data collection?
  • Transparency: Do people know HOW their data is used?
  • Fairness: Does the analysis discriminate against anyone?
  • Accountability: Who is responsible when data causes harm?
  • Minimization: Collect only what's truly needed?

BSIS Advantage

BSIS = Business + Technology. Perfect intersection for data analytics.

You understand business processes AND technology. Data Scientists know the tech but often lack business context. Business people understand the problem but can't code. YOU can do both.
