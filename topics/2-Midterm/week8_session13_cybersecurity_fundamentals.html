What Is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity

The practice of protecting systems, networks, programs, and data from digital attacks, unauthorized access, damage, or theft. It encompasses technology, processes, and people working together to defend digital assets.

Confidentiality

Keep secrets secret. Only people who SHOULD see data CAN see it.

Protection MethodHow It Works
EncryptionScramble data so only key-holders read it
Access controlRequire login + permissions
MFA (Multi-Factor Auth)Password + phone code + biometric
Data classificationLabel data: public, internal, confidential, secret

Integrity

Data hasn't been tampered with. What you read is what was written.

Protection MethodHow It Works
HashingGenerate unique fingerprint of data (SHA-256)
Digital signaturesProve who sent it and it wasn't modified
Version controlTrack all changes with history (Git)
ChecksumsVerify file hasn't been corrupted in transfer

Availability

Systems work when users need them. Downtime = lost money, lost trust.

Protection MethodHow It Works
RedundancyBackup servers ready to take over
DDoS protectionFilter malicious traffic spikes
Regular backupsRestore data after failure
Disaster recovery planDocumented steps to restore service

Phishing: #1 Attack Vector

90% of cyberattacks START with phishing. Fake emails that look real.

  • "Your GCash account has been locked. Click here to verify."
  • "BDO: Unusual login detected. Confirm identity now."
  • "You won ₱50,000! Claim your prize: [link]"
  • "HR: Your salary slip is attached" (malware attachment)
  • All FAKE. All designed to steal your credentials.
16

Spot the Phish

Red FlagWhat to Check

Urgency"Act NOW or your account will be closed"
Generic greeting"Dear Customer" instead of your name
Suspicious linkHover: URL doesn't match the brand
Grammar errorsPoor English, odd phrasing
Too good to be true"You won!" when you never joined anything

Social Engineering

The Human Exploit

Manipulating people into breaking security procedures or revealing confidential information. The cheapest, easiest, and most effective attack because no firewall can protect against human trust.

20

Why Humans Are Weak

  • Trust: we naturally trust people who seem authoritative
  • Fear: urgency makes us act without thinking
  • Greed: "free" offers bypass rational thought
  • Laziness: same password everywhere, skip updates
  • Ignorance: don't recognize threats
  • Helpfulness: want to help the "IT guy" asking nicely

WannaCry (2017)

  • Ransomware that spread like a worm (no click needed)
  • Exploited a Windows vulnerability that Microsoft had ALREADY patched
  • Organizations that didn't update were hit: hospitals, banks, telecom
  • UK hospitals couldn't access patient records for days
  • Lesson: UPDATE YOUR SOFTWARE. The patch existed MONTHS before the attack.
25

COMELEC Data Breach (2016)

  • Philippines' Commission on Elections database hacked
  • 55 MILLION voter records exposed (names, addresses, fingerprints)
  • One of the largest government data breaches in history
  • Biometric data (fingerprints) compromised: PERMANENT damage
  • Showed how vulnerable Philippine government systems are

Personal Security Checklist

  • Use UNIQUE passwords for every account (password manager)
  • Enable MFA/2FA everywhere (SMS or authenticator app)
  • Update OS and apps immediately when patches available
  • Don't click links in unexpected messages (verify first)
  • Use HTTPS websites only for sensitive data
  • Backup important files (3-2-1 rule: 3 copies, 2 media, 1 offsite)

Encryption Basics

Symmetric

  • Same key to lock and unlock
  • Fast but key sharing is risky
  • Like a padlock: same key opens/closes
  • Example: AES-256

Asymmetric

  • Two keys: public (encrypt) + private (decrypt)
  • Slower but more secure for sharing
  • Like a mailbox: anyone drops in, only you open
  • Example: RSA, used in HTTPS
