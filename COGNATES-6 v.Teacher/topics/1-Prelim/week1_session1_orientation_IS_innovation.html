What Is
Innovation?

13Definition

Innovation

Innovation = creating, developing, and implementing new or improved products, services, processes, or business models that deliver meaningful value.

14Distinction

Invention ≠ Innovation

InventionInnovation
Creating something newTurning idea into adopted value
Telephone (Bell)Smartphone
Can sit unusedMust reach users and create impact
15Reality

95%

of new products fail globally. Invention isn't enough.

165 Characteristics

What Makes Something Innovative?

  1. Novelty
  2. Usefulness
  3. Adoption
  4. Value Creation
  5. Scalability
17PH Example

GCash: Innovation in Action

  • Novel: Mobile wallet for unbanked Filipinos
  • Useful: Bills, send money, buy load
  • Adopted: 94M+ users

  • Scalable: Nationwide

21Definition

Information System

IS = organized combination of people, hardware, software, data, and procedures that collects, processes, stores, and distributes information.

22Core Components

5 Parts of IS

People+Hardware+Software+Data+Procedures=IS
23Detailed

Components Explained

ComponentWhatExample
HardwarePhysical devicesServers, PCs, phones
SoftwareProgramsERP, databases, apps
DataRaw factsGrades, transactions
ProceduresRules/workflowsEnrollment steps
PeopleUsers/adminsIT staff, students
24Key Insight

Technology is only ONE piece.
People + Process make or break it.

25Scenario

When Components Are Ignored

❌ Failure

School buys system. No training. No procedures. Back to paper.

✓ Success

Same school trains staff, redesigns process. Adopted in one semester.
