06Type 1

Incremental

Incremental = small, continuous improvements. Low risk. 90% of what companies do.

07Type 2

Sustaining

Sustaining = improvements for EXISTING customers. Maintains position without changing market.

08Type 3

Radical

Radical = completely new approach creating entirely new market. High risk, transformative.

09Type 4

Disruptive

Disruptive = targets overlooked users with simpler/cheaper, then moves upmarket and displaces incumbents.

Key: Starts GOOD ENOUGH, not better.

10Theory

Clayton Christensen's
Disruptive Innovation

11Pattern

How Disruption Works

  1. Entrant appears: simpler for overlooked
  2. Incumbent ignores: "too small"
  3. Entrant improves rapidly
  4. Takes mainstream customers
  5. Incumbent dies or scrambles

18Paradox

Why Big Firms Miss It

  1. Listen to customers → don't want new thing
  2. Focus on profit → new market too small
  3. Protect investments → sunk cost
  4. Move slowly → bureaucracy
  5. Dismiss threats → "they're tiny"

23Test

Truly Disruptive vs Just New

Disruptive ✓

  • Changes market structure
  • New user base
  • Threatens incumbent model
  • Starts simpler/cheaper

Just New ✗

  • Only adds features
  • Same users
  • Easily copied
  • No structural change
24Rule

Quick Test

If incumbents can easily adopt it without changing their business model, it's NOT disruptive.

27Debate

TikTok: Yes vs No

YES

  • Changed content creation
  • Displaced trad media for Gen Z
  • Forced YouTube/IG to copy

NO

  • YouTube existed
  • Same ad model
  • Didn't kill competitors
28Answer

Nuanced Reality

TikTok = partially disruptive. Disrupted content DISCOVERY and LENGTH. Didn't fully displace platforms. More: radical innovation in distribution.

Real world isn't always clean. Frameworks help you THINK.
