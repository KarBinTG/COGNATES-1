Blockchain Solution: Tuna Tracking

  • Fisher in GenSan catches tuna → record on blockchain (who, when, where, weight)
  • Processing plant receives → records handling, temperature, quality check
  • Shipping company → records transport conditions, route
  • Customs/port → records inspection, clearance
  • Restaurant in Tokyo → scans QR code, sees ENTIRE history
  • Consumer confidence: verified fresh, legal, properly handled

Smart Contracts Deep Dive

IF condition met → THEN execute automatically

Self-executing programs stored on blockchain. When predefined conditions are verified, the contract executes automatically. No lawyers, no courts, no delays. The code IS the law.

Smart Contract: OFW Remittance

Scenario: OFW in Saudi sends tuition money for child in Philippines.

Smart Contract: "IF school confirms enrollment AND tuition due date arrives, THEN release ₱30,000 directly to school's account."

Benefit: Money goes directly to school, not to anyone else. OFW has peace of mind. Automatically verified. No middleman takes a cut.

NFTs (Non-Fungible Tokens)

What They Are

  • Unique digital ownership certificates
  • Stored on blockchain (can't be duplicated)
  • Prove YOU own a specific digital asset
  • Can represent: art, music, tickets, deeds

Reality Check

  • 2021 hype: Bored Apes sold for millions
  • 2023 crash: most NFTs lost 90%+ value
  • Useful concept, but overhyped for art
  • Better use: tickets, certificates, deeds

DAOs (Decentralized Autonomous Organizations)

Organization Run by Code, Not Bosses

Members vote on decisions using tokens. Rules are encoded in smart contracts. No CEO, no board. Transparent treasury. Examples: Protocol governance (Uniswap), investment DAOs, charity DAOs. Like a cooperative where code enforces the bylaws.

05 Part 5

Hype vs
Reality

Critical evaluation of blockchain claims

22

Evaluation Framework

  • 1. Is there a REAL trust problem between multiple parties?
  • 2. Do intermediaries add cost/friction that could be removed?
  • 3. Is immutability actually NEEDED (or is editing okay)?
  • 4. Can participants agree on shared rules?
  • 5. Is the speed trade-off acceptable for this use case?
  • 6. Would a regular database solve this more simply?
23

24

The Balanced View

Blockchain IS Good For

  • Cross-border payments
  • Supply chain transparency
  • Digital identity
  • Tamper-proof records
  • Removing expensive middlemen

Blockchain Is NOT For

  • Everything (not a universal solution)
  • High-speed transactions
  • Data that needs editing/deletion
  • Systems where one org is trusted
  • Simple problems (over-engineering)
