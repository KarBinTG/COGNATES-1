What Is Network Slicing?

Virtual Dedicated Networks on Shared Infrastructure

Network slicing divides ONE physical 5G network into multiple virtual networks, each optimized for a specific use case. Like having separate lanes on a highway: one for ambulances (low latency), one for trucks (high bandwidth), one for bikes (low power IoT).

06

Slice Examples

SliceOptimized ForUse Case
Ultra-reliable slice99.999% uptime, 1ms latencyRemote surgery, autonomous vehicles
Broadband sliceMaximum speed (Gbps)Video streaming, XR, downloads
IoT sliceMillions of low-power devicesSmart city sensors, agriculture
Enterprise sliceSecurity + guaranteed performancePrivate corporate network
Emergency slicePriority access during disastersFirst responders, government
07

Why Slicing Matters

Without slicing: a massive concert crowd streaming TikTok could congest the network and delay a nearby ambulance's emergency communication. With slicing: ambulance uses a GUARANTEED separate virtual lane. Concert-goers can't affect it.

Business model: Telcos can sell premium slices to hospitals, factories, and governments at higher prices. Different service levels = different revenue streams from the same infrastructure.

What Is the Digital Divide?

Digital Divide

The gap between those who have access to modern information and communication technologies (internet, devices, digital skills) and those who do not. This divide exists along lines of geography, income, age, education, and gender.

16

Three Levels of Digital Divide

LevelThe GapExample
1st Level: AccessDo you have internet/device at all?Rural barangay with no cell signal
2nd Level: UsageCan you USE technology effectively?Has phone but can't use email/apps
3rd Level: OutcomesDo you BENEFIT from technology use?Uses internet but only for games, not income/education

Emerging Solutions

  • Starlink satellite internet: covers remote islands without towers
  • TV White Space: use unused TV frequencies for rural broadband
  • Community networks: barangay-owned WiFi sharing
  • Offline-first apps: work without constant internet, sync when connected
  • Low-Earth Orbit (LEO) satellites: global coverage by 2030
  • 5G Fixed Wireless: home internet without fiber cables
24

Role of IS Professionals

  • Design systems that work on LOW-bandwidth connections
  • Build offline-capable applications for underserved areas
  • Evaluate technology solutions for EQUITY, not just efficiency
  • Advocate for digital inclusion in project decisions
  • Create solutions in local languages (Filipino, Bisaya)
  • Bridge the gap between tech companies and communities

Activity: Digital Divide Solution

Design for Inclusion

Scenario: A fishing community in Sarangani has NO internet and limited electricity.

Design a technology solution that:
1. Works with minimal/intermittent connectivity
2. Uses affordable devices (not smartphones)
3. Provides real VALUE to fishermen (weather, market prices, safety)
4. Can be maintained locally
5. Is financially sustainable

Think: SMS-based? Radio? Mesh network? Solar-powered? LoRa sensors?
