What Is the Metaverse?

Metaverse

A persistent, shared, 3D virtual world (or network of worlds) where people can socialize, work, play, learn, and transact using avatars. Accessed through XR devices, it blends elements of social media, gaming, AR, VR, and cryptocurrency into a connected digital universe.

Spatial Computing

Apple's Framing: "Spatial Computing"

Instead of "metaverse," Apple calls it spatial computing: using the 3D space around you as your interface. Apps float in your room. You interact with eye tracking and hand gestures. Your physical environment IS the computer screen. Vision Pro is the first mainstream attempt.

When does XR make sense vs traditional methods?

18

XR Evaluation Framework

  • 1. Is the task SPATIAL or EXPERIENTIAL? (If yes, XR adds value)
  • 2. Is physical practice DANGEROUS, EXPENSIVE, or IMPOSSIBLE?
  • 3. Does VISUALIZATION help understanding significantly?
  • 4. Can the target audience ACCESS the required devices?
  • 5. Is the ROI justified vs simpler alternatives (video, 2D)?
  • 6. Are there ETHICAL concerns (privacy, safety, access equity)?
19

XR Makes Sense When...

Good XR Use Cases ✓

  • Surgery training (dangerous to practice on real patients)
  • Disaster drills (can't create real earthquakes)
  • Architecture walkthrough (building doesn't exist yet)
  • Remote collaboration (teams across the world)
  • Phobia therapy (controlled exposure)

XR Is Overkill ✗

  • Reading a textbook (text is fine)
  • Simple data entry training (screen recording works)
  • Basic meetings (Zoom is sufficient)
  • Content that changes frequently (costly to update)
  • Audiences without device access

XR Health Guidelines

  • Take 10-15 minute breaks every hour of VR use
  • Children under 13: limited VR use (brain development concerns)
  • Stop immediately if experiencing nausea or disorientation
  • Ensure adequate physical space (avoid hitting real objects)
  • Monitor for signs of addiction or social withdrawal

Digital Twins

A virtual replica of a physical system

A digital twin is a real-time virtual copy of a physical object, process, or system. Changes in the real world update the digital version, and you can test modifications virtually before applying them physically. Used in: factories, cities, buildings, human organs.

Example: Singapore has a complete digital twin of the entire city for urban planning.
